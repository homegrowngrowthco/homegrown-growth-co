Sales Comp & Enablement

Commission plans that drive the right
behavior — and the modeling to prove it.

Commission plans that drive the right behavior, and onboarding systems that get reps productive faster.

Book Your Free ROI Call View All Services

Bad comp plans are expensive in two directions: reps game incentives that aren't designed carefully, and good reps leave when the plan doesn't feel fair. Most comp problems aren't about the numbers — they're about misalignment between what you're paying for and what you actually need. A well-designed comp plan is a strategy document as much as a finance one. It defines what behavior you're buying and makes sure you're getting it.

What's included

  • Comp plan design: quota-setting, OTE structure, accelerators, and SPIFFs
  • Commission modeling and scenario analysis to stress-test plan economics before rollout
  • Sales onboarding frameworks: ramp structure, enablement content, and certification milestones
  • Handoff documentation and templates for ongoing administration

Good fit if...

Your comp plan is a spreadsheet that hasn't been revisited since you hired your first rep
You're scaling the sales team and need a repeatable ramp program
Reps are hitting quota but not driving the margin or segment mix you need
Common Questions

Frequently asked.

How do you approach quota-setting?

We start with a bottoms-up model: realistic capacity per rep, historical conversion rates, and your revenue targets. Quotas should be attainable by a good rep, not set to what you need the whole team to hit. We model it both ways and find a number that's fair and financially sound.

What if we already have a comp plan — can you just review it?

Yes. A comp plan review is a lighter-weight version of the full engagement: we walk through your current structure, identify the incentive misalignments and administration friction, and recommend specific changes. Typical turnaround is 1–2 weeks.

Do you help with comp administration after the plan is designed?

The engagement ends with documentation and templates your team can run. We don't provide ongoing commission calculation services — the goal is a plan simple enough that you don't need a consultant to run it month to month.

How much does sales comp work cost?

Comp work runs as a retainer engagement, starting at $3,000/month for 5 hours/week and scaling to $10,000/month for 20 hours/week. Plan reviews can be project-scoped. See the full pricing breakdown or book a call to discuss.
